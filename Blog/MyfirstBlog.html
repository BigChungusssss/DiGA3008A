1. Reading

The Reading I have done for the first week is Berners-Lee et al. - 1994 - The World-Wide Web.

Reading Berners-Lee’s, The World-Wide Web feels like looking the first steps into the birth of a new age. In 1994, the Web was an instrument used by scientists to share information. Yet it started to quickly spread beyond academia, thanks to browsers such as Netscape, making the internet accessible to everyone.

The article highlights the Web’s powerful foundation like hypertext, URL’s and HTTP. What seemed extraordinary in the early years of the Web’s life, now second nature, shaping how we communicate, learn and work. But with this sudden growth, emerged problems such privacy and misinformation.

Looking back, it’s incredible to see how one idea transformed the world. The Web started as a tool for sharing knowledge, but today, it defines our digital lives.
